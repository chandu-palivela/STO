| Form Name | Type / Purpose | Brief Description & Example |
|---|---|---|
| Form 16 | Salary Tax Certificate | Issued by your employer. It details your total salary earned, tax deducted (TDS), and exemptions claimed. Example: Your HR emails this to you in June so you can see exactly how much tax was deducted from your monthly salary. |
| Form 12BB | Investment Declaration | Submitted by you to your employer. It lists your planned tax-saving investments (like LIC, PPF, or house rent) to lower your monthly TDS. Example: You submit this in January with rent receipts to ensure your employer does not deduct extra tax. |
| Form 26AS | Tax Credit Statement | Consolidated tax passport viewable on the e-filing portal. It tracks all tax deducted against your PAN by employers, banks, or buyers. Example: You log into the tax portal to verify that the TDS cut by your bank matches your actual interest earned. |
| Form 16A | Non-Salary TDS Certificate | Issued by banks or clients for income other than salary. Example: Your fixed deposit earns high interest, and the bank issues this form showing the 10% TDS they deducted. |
| Form 15G | TDS Avoidance (Under 60) | Declaration filed by individuals under 60 years old to prevent banks from deducting TDS on interest income, provided total income is below the taxable limit. Example: A non-working student submits this to the bank so no tax is cut from their savings interest. |
| Form 15H | TDS Avoidance (Senior Citizens) | Declaration filed by senior citizens (aged 60 or above) to prevent banks from deducting TDS on interest income. Example: A retiree submits this to ensure their monthly pension interest is credited without any tax deductions. |
